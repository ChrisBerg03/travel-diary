Niagara Falls, USA/Canada

Visited: 12/10/2020

Niagara Falls, a natural wonder located on the border of Canada and the United States, is a breathtaking spectacle of three massive waterfalls: Horseshoe Falls, American Falls, and Bridal Veil Falls. With an astonishing flow rate, it's a mesmerizing display of raw power and beauty, drawing millions of visitors each year. The deafening roar of cascading waters and the mist that envelops the area make it an unforgettable experience.

The Great Pyramid of Giza, Egypt

Visited: 06/03/2020

The Great Pyramid of Giza, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, is a colossal architectural marvel situated on the Giza Plateau in Egypt. Built over 4,500 years ago, it stands as a testament to the advanced engineering skills of the ancient Egyptians. Rising majestically from the desert sands, this pyramid served as a tomb for Pharaoh Khufu and continues to captivate the world with its imposing presence and historical significance.

The Great Wall of China

Visited: 10/08/2021

The Great Wall of China is an iconic feat of ancient engineering and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Stretching over 13,000 miles, it winds its way through China's rugged terrain, serving as a historic defensive fortification. Constructed over centuries, it symbolizes China's rich history and enduring cultural legacy, attracting millions of visitors from around the globe to marvel at its grandeur.

Eiffel Tower, France

Visited: 12/10/2022

The Eiffel Tower, an iconic Parisian landmark, stands as an enduring symbol of France's artistic and engineering prowess. Designed by Gustave Eiffel, this wrought-iron masterpiece reaches a height of 324 meters and offers stunning panoramic views of the City of Light from its observation decks. Completed in 1889 for the Exposition Universelle, it continues to captivate visitors with its grace and elegance, making it one of the world's most recognized and beloved structures.

Stonehenge, England

Visited: 02/02/2023

Stonehenge, a prehistoric monument in England, is a mystifying and enigmatic marvel of ancient architecture. Comprising massive standing stones arranged in a circular formation, its origins and purpose continue to intrigue scholars and visitors alike. Dating back over 4,000 years, it is believed to have served as an astronomical observatory or a sacred religious site, drawing people from around the world to ponder its enduring mysteries.
